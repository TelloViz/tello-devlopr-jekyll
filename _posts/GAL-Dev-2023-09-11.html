This is the first official dev log post for Project GAL.

Background and Intentions

Project GAL aims to fill my portfolio's gap in 3D game development. My goal is to enhance my prospects in the video game industry job market.

Development Approach

Originally, the project was a Western-themed bullet hell game, but to ensure a timely completion, I simplified it into a rail-based Western gallery shooter inspired by Disneyland's Toy Story Midway Mania and the arcade classic Time Crisis. With the prefabs and systems I am implementing with this new version, I intend to modify it into the original 3rd person POV concept later on.

Challenges and Issues

Initially, a winding dolly-track navigated a Western settlement. However, this caused camera challenges when the dolly-cart's sudden directional changes instantaneously altered the forward vector of the player/camera. To simplify, I redesigned the travel path as a straight-track with player-controlled first-person aiming from the cart. Players can look around within clamped limits, enhancing immersion. A centered reticle follows the player's mouse-controlled camera motion.

The player character was originally intended to be controlled from a 3rd-person POV, but after considering the complexity of 3D character rigging/animating, I opted to take a 1st person POV approach instead.

Hardware limitations on my aging PC have hampered efficiency. Within Unity, understanding control flow between Editor and Play modes and standalone exe has been a learning curve. I'm exploring different coding patterns, currently using Tarodev's singleton systems and considering a return to a C# native State Machine and custom event system, that I've used in previous projects, for greater code decoupling.
